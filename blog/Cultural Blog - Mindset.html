Neuroplasticity and Growth Mindset

Harnessing Neuroplasticity & Growth Mindset

Neuroplasticity
Neuroplasticity refers to the brain’s ability to adapt. We all have the ability to learn and change, by rewiring our brains. If you have every changed a bad habit or thought about something differently you have carved a new pathway in your brain and experienced neuroplasticity first-hand. With repeated and directed attention towards your desired change you can rewire your brain.The primary driver of change in your brain is your behaviour, so there’s no neuroplasticity drug that you can take. Nothing is more effective than practice at helping you learn and the bottom line is - you have to put in the hard mahi. Increased difficulty/struggle during practice actually leads to both more learning, and greater structural change in the brain. Growth mindset and neuroplasticity conceptually mirror each other; a growth mindset - is a mindset that one’s innate skills, talents, and abilities can be developed and/or improved with determination, while neuroplasticity refers to the brain’s ability to adapt and develop beyond the usual developmental period of childhood. Neuroplasticity can have positive or negative implications, which are determined by your habits. If you’ve cultivated good habits, those neural pathways are strengthened in the brain. In contrast, if you’ve got terrible habits that you’ve done for years, it’ll be harder to break those patterns. I need to keep cultivating these good habits; exercise, eating well, getting enough sleep, maintaining important relationships & putting in the hard mahi into this dev bootcamp.

Growth Mindset
“Ever tried. Ever failed. No matter. Try again. Fail again. Fail better” (Samuel Beckett). Fall down seven times, get up eight. 七転び八起き Nana korobi ya oki. Life is full of ups and downs but you must persevere! When individuals believe they can get smarter, they understand that effort makes them stronger. Therefore they put in extra time and effort, and that leads to higher achievement."The passion for stretching yourself and sticking to it, even (or especially) when it's not going well, is the hallmark of the growth mindset. This is the mindset that allows people to thrive during some of the most challenging times in their lives" (Carol Dweck). It takes time and perseverance to become proficient in programming and numerous other skills associated with being a great developer. Valuable skills take years to cultivate. When things get tough you’ve got to keep going. “Effort is one of those things that gives meaning to life. Effort means you care about something, that something is important to you and you are willing to work for it” (C. Dweck).
